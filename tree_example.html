Kuromatsu - Japanese Black Pine

Illustration Description
Pines have an important role in making Japan what it is today.
Usually the very first tree people associate with bonsai is the extremely popular Kuromatsu.

It is recommended for beginners because it is robust and easy to raise.


Placement Watering Fertilizing Sprout Cutting Pest Damage Transplanting
Place in a well-ventilated and well fit place. It can be raised in partial shade Whenever the surface of the soil is dry give plenty of water Place plenty of solid type fertilizers Cut the lower sprouts in September. Trim the main trunk in April Be careful about aphids, sider mites and leaf withering diseases. Sprinkle the tree with insecticide If it is young do this once every 2 years, old once every three years. The best time is between March & April
